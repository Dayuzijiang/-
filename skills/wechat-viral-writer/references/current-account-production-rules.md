# Current Account Production Rules

Use this reference for topic selection, policy or livelihood writing, visual planning, and WeChat packaging for the current account.

## Audience And Topic Selection

- Core audience: middle-aged readers concerned with family, relationships, money, health, caregiving, and emotional security.
- Include topics relevant to rural areas, county towns, and second- or third-tier cities: farming subsidies, rural pensions, medical reimbursement, homesteads, eldercare, children's education, and county housing.
- Scan current hot topics and official policy sources first. Keep only angles with direct practical value and discussion potential for this audience.
- Avoid consecutive repetitions of the same theme and avoid generic middle-age reflections without a current or practical hook.

## One-Minute Retention Rule

- Assume average reader retention is about one minute.
- Fulfill the title promise within roughly the first 300 Chinese characters.
- For policy or livelihood articles, state first: what changed, who may qualify, what benefit or risk is involved, and where to ask or apply.
- Put family scenes, conflict, interpretation, and emotional discussion after the practical answer.
- Do not hide the named policy or core fact until section three or four.

## Body Image Rules

- For policy, regulation, subsidy, medical, education, or social-news articles, prefer real screenshots from government websites, official institutions, or authoritative media.
- Match one screenshot to each section's exact information. Crop for mobile readability and preserve a recognizable source name or URL.
- A red outline may identify the key original clause. Do not rewrite text and present it as if it were part of the original webpage.
- Use AI lifestyle images only when a suitable authoritative visual is unavailable or when the article is primarily emotional rather than informational.

## Cover Image Rules

- Cover logic is separate from body-image logic. Do not use a full webpage screenshot as the default cover.
- The cover should be a finished poster with the article headline as the dominant element.
- Keep only the headline and a few relevant visual elements. Do not add dates, source labels, URLs, long policy lists, or article excerpts unless explicitly requested.
- Current approved policy-cover direction: deep-blue media-notice poster, blue gradient, subtle corner dots and bottom wave lines, large heavy dimensional white Chinese headline, and one simple topic-related line icon.
- Keep the headline readable at thumbnail size. Avoid decorative lines crossing the text.

## Packaging

- Keep narrow body copy, strong whitespace, short paragraphs, rounded red section-number PNGs, and only a few red emphasis blocks.
- Place one matching horizontal image after each major section.
- Upload the cover only as WeChat cover material; never insert it into the article body.
- Preserve existing draft media IDs when iterating so re-import updates the same draft.
