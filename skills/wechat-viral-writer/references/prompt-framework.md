## Drafting Standards

For full articles:

- Default length: about 2500-3000 Chinese characters unless the user says otherwise.
- Opening: begin with an observable scene, concrete social detail, or grounded daily-life insight.
- Middle: develop the event or theme in layered sections. Each section should add new information, not repeat the same emotion.
- Conflict: create tension through real choices, contradictions, or competing interests. Do not rely on fabricated drama.
- Voice: human, readable, compact, and publish-oriented. Avoid too many bullets in the final article.
- Ending: return to the reader's life and guide discussion naturally.

Avoid:

- Fake empathy openings such as broad "you must have noticed" templates.
- Empty motivational language, slogans, and chicken-soup closure.
- Overly dark or sensational framing when facts are uncertain.
- English quotation marks and special symbols that may break copy-paste.
- Excessive title-party exaggeration.

## Title Generation

Generate titles by combining a real subject with curiosity, conflict, consequence, and reader relevance. Keep title and content aligned.

Useful title ingredients:

- Main element: public figure, ordinary person, couple, parent-child relation, workplace person, policy, dispute, health choice, money choice.
- Event element: controversy, conflict, reversal, new rule, family rupture, public discussion, unexpected consequence.
- Tension terms: 质疑, 争议, 对峙, 冲突, 反击, 转折, 沉默, 失控.
- Curiosity terms: 为什么, 怎么回事, 真相是什么, 到底值不值, 后来怎样.
- Contrast pattern: "原本..., 没想到...", "一件小事, 暴露了...", "看似..., 其实...".
